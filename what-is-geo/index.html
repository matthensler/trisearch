The Definition

Search has a new
third dimension.

GEO — Generative Engine Optimization — is the practice of structuring your business's digital presence so that AI tools recommend you by name when people ask for what you offer.

Traditional SEO gets you ranked in a list of blue links on Google. GEO gets you cited in the answer itself. When someone asks ChatGPT "what's the best [your category] near me?" — the businesses that appear aren't there by accident. They've been optimized to be there.

GEO works differently from SEO. AI engines synthesize information from across the entire web — your website, third-party reviews, news mentions, Reddit, LinkedIn, industry directories — and form an opinion about your brand's authority and relevance.

If those signals don't clearly establish what your business does and why it's credible, you don't get recommended. It's binary. Unlike Google, there's no page 2. The AI either names you or it doesn't.

How AI engines find you

AI tools pull from your website, review platforms, news coverage, Reddit, LinkedIn, and industry publications. Optimization requires working across all of these — not just your homepage.

GEO vs. zero-click search

Even when users don't click through, being named in an AI answer builds brand authority. People remember which businesses AI tools recommend — making GEO a brand-building channel as much as a traffic channel.

The first-mover window

As of 2025, 47% of brands have no GEO strategy. Businesses establishing AI authority now will be significantly harder to displace — the same dynamic that made early SEO adopters so hard to outrank.

How AI Engines Decide Who to Recommend

Six signals that determine
your AI search visibility.

When someone asks ChatGPT or Claude to recommend a business, the AI draws on everything it knows about your brand. These are the six factors that matter most.

🏛️

Domain & Brand Authority

AI engines weight brands that appear frequently across trusted, high-authority sources. Sites with 32K+ referring domains are 3.5x more likely to appear in ChatGPT answers than those with minimal backlink profiles.

We track: Domain authority · Referring domain count · Brand authority index
📝

Content Structure & Clarity

AI tools prefer content that is factual, direct, and structured for extraction. Pages with clear headings, FAQ sections, concise summaries, and schema markup earn significantly higher citation rates.

We track: Schema implementation · Content structure score · FAQ coverage
🔗

Third-Party Citation Quality

AI engines learn from sources they already trust — Wikipedia, Forbes, industry publications, Reddit, Trustpilot, G2. Brands with profiles across multiple trusted platforms are 3x more likely to be recommended.

We track: Review platform presence · Press mention frequency · Citation source authority
🧠

Entity Recognition

AI models build an internal understanding of what your business is, what it does, and who it serves. The clearer and more consistently this is defined across your site and the web, the more confidently AI tools recommend you.

We track: Entity clarity score · NAP consistency · Category signal strength
⚡

Content Freshness

AI platforms prefer content that is on average 25% fresher than what traditional search engines cite. Regularly updated pages and recent press coverage tell AI tools your business is current and relevant.

We track: Average content age · Update frequency · Freshness signal index
💬

Community & Social Proof

Reddit and Quora are among the most-cited sources in AI-generated answers. Brands mentioned thousands of times in community discussions are significantly more likely to appear in AI recommendations.

We track: Reddit mention volume · Quora presence · Community citation rate
